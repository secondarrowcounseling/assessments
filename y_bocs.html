Yale-Brown Obsessive-Compulsive Scale (Y-BOCS)

1. Patient Information & Instructions

OBSESSIONS are unwelcomed and distressing ideas, thoughts, or impulses that repeatedly enter your mind. They may seem to occur against your will. Examples include recurrent impulses to do harm or severe fear of contamination.

COMPULSIONS are behaviors or acts that you feel driven to perform although you may recognize them as senseless or excessive. Examples include checking door locks repeatedly, handwashing, or mental rituals like silent repeating phrases.
